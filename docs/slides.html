The Concept
design brief

The wok is the controller.
The shrimp is you.

Tilt your phone to move. Flick to jump.
Collect MSG. Dodge hazards. Don't get cooked.

The player holds the exact object their character is trapped inside. When you tilt it — you're not pressing a button, you're tilting the world. That's the design argument.

Game Design
3-stage arc

Same Wok, Different Meaning

🍤 Stage 1 — The Shrimp

You're being cooked. Tilt to dodge hazards, collect MSG to progress, grab oil to survive. Wok toss = jump.

✓ Shipped
👨‍🍳 Stage 2 — The Chef's Hand

A giant hand reaches into the wok. Swat it away. Same tilt input, opposite intent — now you're fighting back.

In Design
🔄 Stage 3 — Role Reversal

You become the chef. The game flips its camera. Same controller, same gestures — completely different meaning.

What You Do

↕️
Tilt

Phone tilt = shrimp movement along the wok surface. Smooth analog control via DeviceOrientation API.

🪃
Flick to Jump

Rapid upward flick launches the shrimp. Small arc movement preserved mid-air — feels like a real toss.

💎
Collect MSG

5 MSG crystals = stage complete. Grab oil drops to refill health. Dodge red hazards or lose oil.

How the Server Stays Warm

Render free tier sleeps after 15 minutes of inactivity. This is how we defeated that.

🤖 UptimeRobot

Pings /health every 5 minutes. Free. No response-size limit. Only checks HTTP 200 — not the body. Render never reaches the 15-min sleep threshold.

✓ Live
💾 Upstash Redis

Leaderboard stored as a JSON blob at key sfr:scores. On every score submission, data is written to Redis. On server restart, it loads back. Scores never reset.
