The Design Argument
"The player holds the exact object
their character is trapped inside.
When you tilt it — you're not pressing
a button. You're tilting the world."

A wok toss is the game's core power move because tossing food in a wok is exactly what you'd do to cook a shrimp. The controller and the narrative say the same thing.

Three-Act Arc — Same Wok, Different Meaning
01
The Shrimp

You're inside the wok being cooked. Tilt to dodge hazards. Collect MSG to progress. Grab oil to survive. Flick up to jump.

The wok tilting feels inevitable — because it is.

Shipped
02
The Chef's Hand

A hand reaches into the wok. Swat it away. Same tilt input, opposite intention — now you're fighting the cook who's cooking you.

The same gesture now means something different.

Designed
03
Role Reversal

You become the chef. The camera flips. Same controller. Same gestures. Completely different character.

One input device. Three moral positions.

Stage 1 Mechanics

What
you do.

Three input primitives. One hand. Zero buttons pressed.

01
Tilt to move

DeviceOrientation gamma/beta axes → normalized tiltX/tiltY → shrimp position on wok. Fully analog. No dead zone below calibration threshold.

02
Flick up to jump

Rapid acceleration on beta axis triggers jump. A small arc drift is preserved mid-air — because a real toss isn't perfectly vertical. (We argued about this for a while.)

03
Collect MSG, dodge hazards

5 MSG crystals = stage complete. Oil drops refill health. Red hazards drain it. A full playthrough takes roughly 30–90 seconds. The leaderboard sorts by time.
