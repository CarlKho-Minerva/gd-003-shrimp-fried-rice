# 02/08/2026 - Brainstorm: Shrimp Fried Rice Variations

Sticking with the **Shrimp Fried Rice** theme + **Wok Controller** but exploring 5 different gameplay styles.

## 1. "Wok Sumo" (Physics Brawler)
*   **Concept:** You are the **Head Shrimp**. Other ingredients (peas, ham chunks, rival shrimp) are trying to push you out of the "perfect heat zone" (center).
*   **Loop:** Use **tosses** to launch enemies into the air and **tilts** to slide them into the "cold edges" (out of bounds).
*   **Why GDC:** Multiplayer potential (1v1 or 1vMany). High energy, competitive shouting. "Get out of my wok!"

## 2. "Sticky Rice Katamari" (Collection Sim)
*   **Concept:** You are a sticky shrimp. You must roll around the wok collecting lost grains of rice and peas until you form the **Ultimate Fried Rice Ball**.
*   **Loop:** Start small, avoid big spatula swipes. As you grow, you can roll up larger items (garlic cloves, then egg chunks).
*   **Why GDC:** Very visual and satisfying physical connection—tilting the wok equates to rolling the ball.

## 3. "Aerial Fryer" (Juggling Survival)
*   **Concept:** The wok surface is **LAVA** (way too hot). You burn if you touch it for >1 second.
*   **Loop:** You must keep the shrimp *in the air* by constantly **tossing** the wok. While airborne, you tilt to guide its landing onto "safe" ingredients (like a cooling egg raft) before tossing again immediately.
*   **Why GDC:** Pure skill/flow state. Spectators watch you frantically tossing a real wok. High spectacle.

## 4. "Rhythm Chef" (Music/Rhythm)
*   **Concept:** Cooking is rhythm. Ingredients fall to the beat.
*   **Loop:** **Toss** on the snare. **Shake** on the hi-hat. **Rotate** on the bass. If you miss a beat, the dish burns.
*   **Why GDC:** Rhythm games are always a hit. The sound of the physical wok sensors matching the music creates a unique "instrument."

## 5. "Stealth Shrimp" (Tension/Horror)
*   **Concept:** You are a raw shrimp trying to cook yourself at night without waking the Chef.
*   **Loop:** Gently **tilt** to slide across the hot metal. If you move too fast = *CLANG* sound = Chef wakes up (Game Over). If you stay still too long = Burn on one side.
*   **Why GDC:** A "quiet" tension game in a loud expo hall is a unique draw. Focuses on delicate, precise control rather than frantic motion.
