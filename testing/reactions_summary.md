# Player Reactions & Feedback (Shrimp Fried Rice)

Based on video testing sessions.

## Core Mechanics & Calibration
- **Mechanics Understanding**: Players generally grasp the core "flicking" and "jumping" mechanics quickly. The concept of keeping the shrimp moving to avoid burning (yellow bar for health/oil, white MSG to progress) makes sense to them. (Jack, Video 1 & 5).
- **Physical Integration**: Players enjoy the physical aspect of flicking their phone like a wok. The calibration phase helps dial in their specific movement speed (Video 3).
- **Engagement**: Players react with physical excitement and sometimes panic (Laryssa, Video 4) when trying to keep up with the game.

## Boss Mechanics & UI
- **Chef Boss**: In Stage 2, players encounter the "Chef" and must actively defend by tapping red hand icons trying to steal their ingredients/oil. This introduces a fun defensive mini-game (Video 2, Video 9).
- **UI Clarity**: Yellow = Oil/Health. White = MSG. Tapping/Flicking is distinguished.

## Technical Performance
- **Cross-Device Performance**: The game (WebGL/HTML5 using phone accelerometers) performs smoothly across different modern and older devices, including the iPhone 16 and a smaller iPhone SE 2024 (Video 6, Video 8).

## Overall Reception
- **Fun Factor**: Testers reported having a lot of fun. The "wok" premise combined with frantic physical movement leads to great energetic reactions. Players like Jack came back for a second round (Video 10).
- **Instruction Clarity**: Many testers were vocal about the need for clearer instructions. The developer clarified that this build was primarily testing the backend and core mechanics, but acknowledged the feedback for future UI/UX updates.
- **Developer Sentiment**: The developer is thrilled that the core mechanics work and is enjoying watching the playtesters' animated reactions.
